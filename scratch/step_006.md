# Step 006: Small Group Discussion Questions for Youth Camp

Adopted Persona: **Compassionate Pastor**

This step designs structured small group discussion questions for our 20 camp participants (divided into 3 groups of 6-7 people). The questions follow the three-tier inductive Bible study method: Observation, Interpretation, and Application.

---

## 1. Questions for Romans 12:1-2 (Identity and Peer Pressure)

### Tier 1: Observation Questions (What does the text say?)
1. In Romans 12:1, what does Paul urge the readers to do with their bodies? What adjectives does he use to describe this sacrifice?
2. What are the two contrasting commands in Romans 12:2? (Look closely at the verbs "conformed" and "transformed").
3. According to Romans 12:2, how does this transformation take place? What is the ultimate purpose of this renewal?

### Tier 2: Interpretation Questions (What does the text mean?)
1. What is the theological significance of Paul saying "by the mercies of God" (Romans 12:1) before giving commands? Why does our obedience depend on God's mercy rather than our own efforts?
2. How does understanding the Greek distinction between *syschematizo* (outward mold/conformed) and *metamorphoo* (inner nature/transformed) help us understand what Paul is asking of us?
3. What does it mean for a sacrifice to be "living"? How is this different from the Old Testament sacrifices, and what does it tell us about Christian worship?

### Tier 3: Application Questions (How does this apply to us?)
1. In what specific areas of your high school or social life do you feel the strongest pressure to "conform" or blend in? (Think about music, social media, slang, or relationships).
2. What does a "renewed mind" look like in the digital age? How can we actively renew our minds instead of letting our phone screens conform us?
3. How does knowing that Jesus was the ultimate sacrifice for our sins free us from the pressure of trying to prove our worth to others?

---

## 2. Questions for Philippians 4:6-7 (Trading Anxiety for Peace)

### Tier 1: Observation Questions (What does the text say?)
1. What is the first command given in Philippians 4:6 regarding anxiety?
2. What are the three components of prayer mentioned in Philippians 4:6 that we are to use "in everything"?
3. In Philippians 4:7, what is promised to happen when we bring our requests to God? What two parts of the person will be guarded?

### Tier 2: Interpretation Questions (What does the text mean?)
1. Paul tells us to pray "with thanksgiving" (Philippians 4:6). Why is thanksgiving so vital when we are in the middle of a stressful situation? How does gratitude change our perspective?
2. The Greek word for "guard" (*phroureo*) is a military term. What image would this have evoked for the Philippian believers living in a Roman garrison city, and how does it apply to our mental state?
3. How does the peace of God "surpass all understanding" (Philippians 4:7)? Why is it different from the peace that the world offers (cf. John 14:27)?

### Tier 3: Application Questions (How does this apply to us?)
1. What are the top things in your "backpack" of worry right now? (e.g., college applications, family drama, fear of failure, loneliness). Why is it so hard to hand them over to God?
2. How can we practically practice "supplication" (crying out in our helplessness) when we feel overwhelmed by panic or stress?
3. How can our small group support and pray for one another this week when we feel anxious?
