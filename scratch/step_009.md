## 1. Survey of Step Files (Research Summary)

### Scripture & Context (Step 001, Step 002)
- **Romans 12:1-2**: Paul calls for complete, logical (*logiken*) devotion to God in light of His massive grace and mercy (detailed in Romans 1–11). Believers present their bodies as living sacrifices, resisting the outward mold of this age (*schema*) and embracing an organic inner transformation (*morphe*) via a renewed mind.
- **Philippians 4:6-7**: Written from Roman prison, Paul commands believers to reject paralyzing worry (*merimnao*), transforming anxiety into worshipful prayer (*proseuche*), specific pleas for help (*deesis*), and grateful praise (*eucharistia*). God's supernatural peace acts as a military garrison (*phroureo*) guarding our hearts and thoughts.
- **Key Commentaries**: MacArthur highlights that *schema* is a temporary outward masquerade (fitting peer pressure), while *morphe* is permanent inner character. Barnes notes that *phroureo* represents a protective garrison guarding the city walls, which is a perfect metaphor for mental health and worry.

### Original Language Analysis (Step 003, Step 004)
- **Romans 12:2**:
  - *Syschematizesthe*: Present passive imperative ("stop being conformed"). Focuses on superficial shaping from external pressure.
  - *Metamorphousthe*: Present passive imperative ("continue to be transformed"). A "divine passive"—the Holy Spirit's organic work of sanctification from the inside out.
  - *Anakainosis*: Qualitative renewal. The instrument of transformation is a renovated mind, thinking in alignment with Scripture.
- **Philippians 4:6-7**:
  - *Merimnate*: Fractured, divided mind pulling in opposite directions (anxiety).
  - *Proseuche* / *Deesis*: Worshipful communion and specific cry of personal need/insufficiency.
  - *Phrouresei*: Future active indicative verb ("will guard/keep watch over" like a soldier standing sentry).

### Devotion drafts, Questions, & Applications (Step 005, Step 006, Step 007, Step 008)
- **Devotion 1 (Romans 12:1-2)**: Uses the illustration of "The Chameleon Trap." Connects peer pressure, screen time, and self-worth to original Greek insights (*syschematizo* vs. *metamorphoo*). Focuses on finding identity in Christ's ultimate sacrifice.
- **Devotion 2 (Philippians 4:6-7)**: Uses the illustration of "The Backpack of Worry" (heavy rocks). Connects stress and anxiety about future planning to the military garrison of peace (*phroureo*). Points to Christ carrying our ultimate burdens on the cross.
- **Discussion Questions**: Three tiers (Observation, Interpretation, Application) designed for small groups of 6-7 teenagers.
- **Daily Applications**: Active screen-time reduction, conformity audits, worry-to-prayer swaps, and gratitude journals.
- **Prayers**: Structured in ACTS format (Adoration, Confession, Thanksgiving, Supplication) written in the first person.
