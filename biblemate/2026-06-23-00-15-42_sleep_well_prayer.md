# Scriptural Prayer for Peaceful Rest and Sleep

## A Pastoral Word of Comfort

Beloved, as the day comes to a close, it is easy for our minds to remain active with the worries, responsibilities, and strivings of this life. But our Heavenly Father desires to give us rest. Sleep is not just a physical necessity; it is a spiritual act of trust. By closing our eyes, we declare that God is in control, that the universe is held in His hands, and that we can step out of our striving and into His grace.

Let these words from Scripture remind you of His watch care over you tonight.

---

## Scriptural Foundation

### Psalms 4:8 (NET)
> "I will lie down and sleep peacefully, for you, Lord, make me safe and secure."

### Psalms 127:2 (NET)
> "It is vain for you to rise early, come home late, and work so hard for your food. Yes, he provides for those whom he loves even when they sleep."

### Proverbs 3:24 (NET)
> "When you lie down you will not be filled with fear; when you lie down your sleep will be pleasant."

---

## First-Person Prayer for Rest

Heavenly Father, 

As I prepare to lie down tonight, I bring the work, the worries, and the thoughts of this day and place them at the foot of Your throne. I confess that my heart is often prone to worry, and my mind struggles to find peace. Forgive me for the moments today when I tried to carry my burdens in my own strength.

Your Word says in **Psalms 127:2** that it is vain for me to rise up early and stay up late, working so hard in anxiety, because You provide for those You love even when they sleep. Tonight, I choose to receive that love. I surrender my to-do list, my unresolved questions, and my cares into Your capable hands. I trust that You are working on my behalf even while my body rests.

Father, protect my mind and my heart as I close my eyes. According to Your promise in **Proverbs 3:24**, let me lie down without fear, and let my sleep be sweet and pleasant. Remove any anxious thoughts, bad dreams, or restless strivings. Instead, let Your Holy Spirit wrap me in Your deep comfort and peace.

I declare with the Psalmist in **Psalms 4:8** that I will lie down and sleep peacefully, for You alone, Lord, make me safe and secure. You never sleep nor slumber. You are my shield, my fortress, and my peace. 

Thank You for Your grace that has carried me through this day, and for the mercies that will be new when I awake. I pray this in the comforting and secure name of my Savior, Jesus Christ.

Amen.

---

## Evening Reflection Questions

1. What specific care or worry from today do I need to explicitly name and release to the Lord before closing my eyes?
2. In what ways have I been striving in my own strength today, and how can I practice resting in God's provision tomorrow?
3. Meditate on the truth that God is fully awake and ruling the world while you sleep. How does this reality bring freedom to your rest?
